★ Countries To Watch

The six nations every football fan should have their eyes on at the 2026 World Cup.

01 / 06 ★★★★★
Defending Champions

Argentina

The reigning World Cup champions are back to defend their crown. With Lionel Messi leading the charge and a squad packed with Premier League and La Liga talent, La Albiceleste remain the team to beat. Current South American Champions and three-time World Cup winners — they know how to win when it matters most.

02 / 06 ★★★★★
World Class Squad

France

Consistently performing on the biggest stage, Les Bleus have achieved the overall best squad depth in the entire world. With Mbappé, Griezmann, and a spine of elite European club talent, France are always dangerous. Two World Cup titles and a constant Finals presence proves this team simply never knows how to disappear.

03 / 06
Ronaldo's Last Dance

Portugal

As this is Cristiano Ronaldo's final World Cup, the whole world has its eyes on Portugal. The legend's last dance combined with the infusion of fresh blood — Félix, Leão, and Neves — makes this a fascinating team to follow. A nation chasing its first World Cup title with the greatest player in its history leading from the front one last time.

04 / 06 ★★★★★
5× World Champions

Brazil

The most successful nation in World Cup history returns hungry after years without the ultimate prize. With Vinícius Jr. at the peak of his powers and a new generation of Seleção talent, Brazil carry the weight of a nation desperate to reclaim their throne. The beautiful game flows through their veins — they are always a threat.

05 / 06 ★★★★★
Tiki-Taka Reborn

Spain

La Roja are back with a breathtaking new generation. Yamal, Pedri, and Morata lead a side that won Euro 2024 in stunning fashion. Spain's possession-based philosophy is once again the envy of the world and with the youngest squad in the tournament, they have both the quality and the hunger to go all the way in 2026.

06 / 06 ★★★★★
4× World Champions

Germany

Die Mannschaft are a team on a mission after struggling in recent tournaments. Under fresh leadership with Florian Wirtz orchestrating from midfield, Germany look revitalised and dangerous. Four World Cup titles remind the world what this nation is capable of, and with a point to prove on home soil, do not count them out in 2026.
